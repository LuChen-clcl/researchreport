## Key Technological Insights

### 1. Multi-Modal Sensing Emergence
The most significant trend is the shift from single-parameter sensing to **multi-modal capabilities**. Recent papers demonstrate sensors that can simultaneously detect pressure, temperature, flow direction, and vibration, mimicking the complexity of biological sensory systems.

### 2. Energy Autonomy
**Self-powered sensing** has emerged as a critical capability, with triboelectric and piezoelectric approaches enabling sensors that don't require external power sources. This is particularly important for wearable and implantable applications.

### 3. Biological Fidelity
Advanced fabrication techniques now enable **micro-scale precision** in replicating biological structures, including tilted microhair arrays, hair-follicle architectures, and lateral line systems inspired by fish.

### 4. Intelligence Integration
Machine learning algorithms are increasingly integrated at the **sensor level**, enabling real-time signal processing, noise reduction, and pattern recognition directly within the sensing system.
